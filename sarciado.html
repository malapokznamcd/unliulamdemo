Fish Sarciado

A comforting Filipino classic made by simmering crispy fried fish in a vibrant sauce of sautéed tomatoes, onions, and garlic. The addition of beaten eggs creates a soft, silky texture that beautifully coats the fish, turning a simple fry into a gourmet meal.

Crispy fried fish perfectly smothered in a rich tomato and egg sauce.
30 MINS Total Time
Easy Difficulty
2 People Serves

Ingredients

  • 1 whole fish (Tilapia, Bangus, or Galunggong)
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 3 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bunch green onions, chopped
  • 1 1/4 cups water
  • 3/4 cup cooking oil (for frying)
  • 4g Maggi Magic Sarap
  • Fish sauce and black pepper to taste

The Art of the Sarsa

The term Sarciado is derived from the Spanish word salsa, which means sauce. In Filipino households, this dish is the gold standard for repurposing leftover fried fish. By creating a fresh tomato and egg base, yesterday's dry fish is transformed into a moist, flavorful, and revitalized meal.

Tomato Base

The acidity of the tomatoes balances the oiliness of the fried fish, while the natural sugars provide a subtle sweetness to the gravy.

Velvety Texture

The key to a perfect Sarciado is the eggs. They should be stirred in quickly so they form soft ribbons rather than large, hard chunks.

Fish Variety

While Tilapia is popular, this recipe works with any firm-fleshed fish like Bangus (milkfish) or Galunggong (mackerel scad).
