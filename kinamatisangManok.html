Kinamatisang Manok

A comforting Filipino chicken stew where tender meat is simmered in a rich, vibrant tomato-based broth. This healthy and hearty dish balances the natural sweetness and acidity of fresh tomatoes with savory notes of fish sauce and ginger.

Tender chicken pieces submerged in a delightfully flavorful tomato stew.
40 MINS Total Time
Easy Difficulty
5 People Serves

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 4 tomatoes, sliced into wedges
  • 1 Knorr Chicken Cube
  • 2 bunches bok choy
  • 3 long green chili peppers
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 3 thumbs ginger, crushed
  • 3 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 2 cups water
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Fish sauce and ground black pepper to taste

The Golden Apple of Pinoy Cuisine

Kinamatisang Manok (also known as Sarciadong Manok) highlights the versatility of the tomato. While traditionally associated with fish (Sarciadong Isda), using chicken transforms this into a hearty, protein-rich stew that is a staple in many Filipino households for its simplicity and nutritional value.

The Two-Stage Tomato

Adding tomatoes in two batches ensures that some melt into the broth for flavor, while others remain intact for texture and freshness.

Sarciado Heritage

The name comes from "sarsa," meaning thick sauce. It is the Filipino equivalent of the Spanish salsa, but treated as a main braising liquid.

Vegetable Boost

Bok choy and green chilies aren't just for flavor; they add essential vitamins and a mild kick that balances the tartness of the tomatoes.
