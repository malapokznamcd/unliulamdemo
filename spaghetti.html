Pinoy Spaghetti

This simple dish will surely be a hit with the family. It combines the flavors of ground pork with the rich flavor of tomatoes and cheese.

Sweet, meaty, and cheesy Filipino-style spaghetti.
30 MINS Cooking Time
Easy Difficulty
6 People Serves

Ingredients

  • 400 grams spaghetti
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 pc onion
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • ½ kg ground pork
  • 2 pcs Knorr pork or beef broth cubes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 500 grams tomato sauce
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • Grated cheese for topping

A Taste of History

While traditional Italian spaghetti is savory and herbal, Pinoy Spaghetti is a unique fusion born from necessity and local preference. Its origins trace back to the mid-20th century, heavily influenced by the presence of American troops in the Philippines. During World War II, a shortage of tomato supplies led locals to experiment with banana ketchup, giving the dish its iconic sweetness and bright red hue.

The Birthday Standard

In Filipino culture, spaghetti is a staple at children's birthday parties. It is often served alongside fried chicken and hotdogs as a symbol of "long life."

The Hotdog Factor

Authentic Pinoy Spaghetti almost always features sliced red hotdogs. This adds a salty contrast to the sweet sauce that kids (and kids-at-heart) love.

Cheese Galore

Unlike the Parmesan used in Italy, Filipinos prefer processed "quick-melt" cheddar or eden-style cheese, which provides a creamy, salty finish.
