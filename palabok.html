Easy Palabok

This Easy Palabok is as simple as it can get but delivers a delicious, authentic result. It features thin rice noodles topped with a rich, savory golden sauce flavored with smoked fish and shrimp, finished with classic Filipino garnishes like crushed chicharon and toasted garlic.

Golden rice noodles smothered in a rich shrimp and smoked fish sauce.
30 MINS Total Time
Easy Difficulty
3 People Serves

Ingredients

  • 8 oz. bihon (rice sticks)
  • 3/4 cup ground pork
  • 12 pieces cooked shrimp
  • 1/4 cup tinapa (smoked fish) flakes
  • 2 cups annatto water
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 piece Knorr Shrimp Cube
  • 1/4 cup crushed chicharon
  • 1/4 cup toasted garlic
  • 2 boiled eggs, sliced
  • Patis and white pepper to taste

Arrange bihon in a deep tray. Pour hot water until submerged and soak for 12-15 minutes. Drain and set aside.
2
Heat oil and SautéTo cook food quickly in a minimal amount of fat over medium-high heat. garlic and onion. Add ground pork and cook until light brown.
3
Add tinapa flakes. Gradually add flour and cook while stirring for 5 minutes over low to medium heat to create a RouxA mixture of fat and flour used as a thickening agent for sauces..
4
Gradually pour in annatto water. Stir constantly until the sauce reaches a smooth, thick consistency.
5
Melt in the shrimp cube. Season with patis and pepper. Pour over the bihon and top with shrimp, chicharon, eggs, and garlic.

The Palabok Tradition

Pancit Palabok is named after the word "palabok," which refers to the colorful garnishes and the rich sauce layered over the noodles. This version is designed to resemble the popular style found in Filipino fast-food chains, prioritizing speed by using bihon noodles while maintaining the deep, smoky flavor of traditional tinapa.

Smoky Secret

Tinapa (smoked fish) flakes are the "soul" of the sauce. Without them, the dish loses its distinctive Filipino profile.

Golden Hue

The iconic orange color is derived naturally from Annatto seeds (Achuete), providing an earthy tone without synthetic dyes.

The Topping Crunch

A perfect Palabok must have texture. The crunch of the chicharon and toasted garlic provides a vital contrast to the soft noodles.
