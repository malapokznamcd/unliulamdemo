Creamy Pininyahang Manok

A festive yet comforting Filipino chicken stew. This dish features tender chicken pieces simmered in pineapple juice, finished with a rich and silky cream sauce that perfectly balances sweetness and savoriness.

Smooth and rich chicken stew with pineapple chunks and colorful bell peppers.
50 MINS Total Time
Easy Difficulty
4-6 People Serves

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 20 oz. canned pineapple chunks (reserve juice)
  • 200 ml Carnation Everyday Creamer (or heavy cream)
  • 2 medium potatoes, diced
  • 2 medium carrots, cut into wedges
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 pieces tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 piece onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp garlic, minced
  • Fish sauce (patis) and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup cooking oil (for frying/sautéing)

A Tropical Classic

Pininyahang Manok is a testament to the Filipino love for fruit-infused savory dishes. While the traditional version is often a light, clear stew, the "Creamy" variation has become a modern favorite for parties and gatherings, offering a more indulgent mouthfeel that pairs perfectly with steamed rice.

The Creaminess Secret

Using a high-fat creamer or evaporated milk prevents the sauce from curdling when it reacts with the acidic pineapple juice.

Veggie Tip

Frying the potatoes and carrots beforehand prevents them from getting mushy and helps them hold their shape during the simmering process.

Variety Matters

You can substitute the dairy with coconut milk (gata) for an even richer, more traditional tropical flavor profile.
