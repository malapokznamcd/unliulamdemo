Chicken Sotanghon Soup with Patola

A comforting Filipino noodle soup featuring tender chicken, silky mung bean vermicelli, and the subtle sweetness of patola (loofah). Perfect for rainy days or a cozy family meal.

Warm, savory, and soothing Filipino chicken noodle soup.
30 MINS Cooking Time
Easy Difficulty
6 People Serves

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 lbs chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 1 piece patola, peeled and sliced crosswise
  • 5 oz sotanghon noodles
  • 8 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1 piece onion, chopped
  • 1 tbsp chicken powder
  • 1.5 quarts water
  • 3 tbsp annatto oil
  • 1 tbsp roasted garlic (for topping)
  • 2 tbsp green onions, chopped (for garnish)
  • Fish sauce and ground black pepper to taste

A Taste of Comfort

Chicken Sotanghon Soup is a quintessential Filipino "comfort food." Unlike heavy stews, this soup is prized for its clear, clean broth and the slippery texture of mung bean vermicelli. The addition of Patola (Sponge Gourd) adds a natural, gentle sweetness that balances the savory chicken base.

The Annatto Glow

The iconic orange-yellow hue of the soup comes from annatto (atsuete) oil, which provides a mild earthy flavor and a warm, inviting color.

"Glass" Noodles

Sotanghon are often called glass noodles because they become completely translucent once cooked. They are excellent at absorbing the flavors of the broth.

Rainy Day Staple

In the Philippines, this soup is the go-to remedy for the rainy season or when someone is feeling under the weather, thanks to its soothing properties.
