Cheesy Pork Caldereta

An upgraded Filipino classic using tender pork ribs simmered in a rich tomato sauce. This version is made extra special with the addition of creamy cheddar cheese and savory liver spread, creating a thick, velvety stew that's perfect with warm rice.

Rich, meaty pork ribs blanketed in a savory, cheesy tomato gravy.
1 HR 10 MINS Total Time
Medium Difficulty
5 People Serves

Ingredients

  • 3 lbs. pork ribs (or shoulder/belly)
  • 1/2 cup cheddar cheese, grated
  • 1/4 cup liver spread
  • 8 oz. tomato sauce
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 potatoes & 2 carrots, diced
  • 1 red & 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 3/4 cup green peas
  • 1 Knorr Pork Cube
  • 1 tsp Knorr Liquid Seasoning
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 onion, chopped & 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups water
  • Salt & black pepper to taste

A Royal Filipino Stew

The name Caldereta comes from the Spanish word caldera, meaning cauldron. Traditionally a goat meat stew, Filipinos have adapted it over the years to use beef, chicken, and pork. This "Cheesy" version reflects the Filipino love for rich, creamy textures, transforming a rustic stew into a decadent feast.

The Cheese Factor

Adding cheese doesn't just add flavor; it acts as a natural thickener, giving the sauce a velvety consistency that clings perfectly to the meat.

The Liver Spread Secret

The "secret" ingredient in authentic Caldereta is liver spread. It provides a deep, earthy umami base that balances the acidity of the tomatoes.

Air-Fried Crunch

Air-frying or pan-frying the potatoes and carrots beforehand prevents them from becoming mushy during the simmering process, keeping the textures distinct.
