Sinigang na bangus

A quintessential Filipino comfort food, this dish features fresh milkfish simmered in a perfectly tart tamarind broth. Packed with healthy vegetables like kangkong and okra, it offers a refreshing and savory sourness that is best enjoyed with warm steamed rice.

A refreshing, sour tamarind broth with tender milkfish and native vegetables.
30 MINS Cooking Time
Easy Difficulty
4-6 People Serves

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. bangus (cleaned and sliced)
  • 40g Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc Mix
  • 1 bunch kangkong (river spinach)
  • 12 pcs sitaw (cut into 2-inch pieces)
  • 6 pcs okra
  • 2 pcs siling haba (green pepper)
  • 1 medium tomato (wedged)
  • 1 medium onion (wedged)
  • 2 ½ tbsp fish sauce (patis)
  • 2 quarts water

Fish

Cook

1
Heat a pot and pour in the water. Add tomatoes and onions, then bring to a boil.
2
Add the bangus slices. Cover and cook over medium heat for about 8 to 12 minutes.
3
Stir in the Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc Mix and cook for another 2 minutes.
4
Add the green peppers, sitaw, and okra. Cover and simmerCooked gently in liquid just below the boiling point to soften and absorb flavors. for 5 to 7 minutes until vegetables are tender.
5
Season with fish sauce and black pepper. Add the kangkong on top, turn off the heat, and cover for 5 minutes before serving.

The Soul of the Islands

Sinigang is often considered the true "national dish" by many Filipinos because of its adaptability to any region. While Bangus (Milkfish) is the most common protein, the souring agent changes based on what grows nearby—from tamarind (sampaloc) in Luzon to guava (bayabas) or even watermelon.

Milkfish Mastery

Bangus is the National Fish of the Philippines. The belly fat (the dark part) is the most coveted piece in a bowl of Sinigang!

Natural Sour

Before "instant mix" packets existed, Filipinos would boil fresh tamarind pulp and mash it through a sieve to get that perfect zing.
