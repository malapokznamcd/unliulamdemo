A Taste of Tradition

Beef Caldereta (or Kaldereta) is a beloved Filipino main course traditionally served at fiestas and special occasions. Derived from the Spanish word caldera (cauldron), this dish was originally made with goat meat. Today, beef is the most popular choice, prized for its ability to become "fall-off-the-bone" tender after hours of simmering.

The Liver Spread Secret

What sets Caldereta apart from other tomato stews (like Afritada or Menudo) is the addition of liver spread, which gives the sauce its signature thick consistency and deep, earthy flavor.

Spanish Influence

The use of ingredients like olives, bell peppers, and red wine highlights the strong Spanish influence on Filipino cuisine during the colonial era.

Creamy Cheese

Modern Filipino Caldereta often includes grated cheese melted directly into the sauce, providing a creamy contrast to the acidity of the tomatoes.
