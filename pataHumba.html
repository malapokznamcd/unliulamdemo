Pata Humba (Braised Pork Hocks)

A celebratory Visayan-style braised pork dish. Pork hocks are slowly simmered in a complex blend of soy sauce, vinegar, and pineapple juice until the meat is fall-off-the-bone tender. Accented with salted black beans, star anise, banana blossoms, and peanuts for a unique savory-sweet profile.

Rich, glossy pork hocks braised to perfection with traditional spices and peanuts.
1.5 - 2 HRS Total Time
Intermediate Difficulty
6 People Serves

Ingredients

  • 4.5 lbs pork hocks, sliced
  • 3 tbsp cooking oil
  • 2 yellow onions, minced
  • 8 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp salted black beans, rinsed
  • 4 pieces star anise
  • 3 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 2 tsp whole peppercorns
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 3 tbsp vinegar
  • 3 cups pineapple juice
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup dried banana blossoms (optional)
  • 1/4 cup peanuts
  • 5g Maggi Magic Sarap (optional)

A Visayan Heritage Dish

Humba is a treasure of Visayan cuisine. While it shares ingredients with Adobo, it is distinguished by its sweeter profile and the addition of fermented black beans (tausi) and star anise. Historically, it was a way to preserve meat, but it has evolved into a centerpiece dish for town fiestas and family reunions.

The Umami Mash

Mashing the salted black beans directly into the oil ensures their deep umami flavor blends into the sauce rather than staying in whole, salty bursts.

Collagen is Key

Pata (pork hock) is used because its high collagen content melts during the long braise, creating a naturally thick, glossy, and silky sauce.

Better with Time

Like many Filipino stews, Pata Humba tastes even better the next day as the meat continues to absorb the caramel and spice notes from the sauce.
