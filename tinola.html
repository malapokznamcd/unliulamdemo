Chicken Tinola

A classic Filipino ginger-based soup that is both healthy and heart-warming. It features tender chicken pieces simmered with green papaya and fresh leafy greens like malunggay and pepper leaves.

Savory chicken soup with a soothing ginger-infused broth.
1 HOUR Cooking Time
Easy Difficulty
6 People Serves

Ingredients

  • 3 lbs. chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 2 green papaya, wedged
  • 1 ½ cup malunggay leaves
  • 1 cup hot pepper leaves
  • 5 cloves garlic, crushed and chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 thumbs ginger, julienned
  • 2 Maggi Magic Chicken Cubes
  • 1 quart rice wash
  • 1 quart water
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Ground black pepper to taste

A Taste of History

Chicken Tinola is a cornerstone of Filipino home cooking. Traditionally made with native chicken, it is valued for its healing properties, often served to breastfeeding mothers or anyone needing a nutritional boost. The secret to a great Tinola lies in the slow simmering of the chicken and the pungent warmth of fresh ginger.

The Rice Wash Secret

Using the water from washing rice (hugas-bigas) as the soup base adds a subtle starchiness and nutrient density that regular water lacks.

Papaya vs. Sayote

The choice between green papaya and sayote is a classic debate. Papaya offers a softer texture and enzymes that tenderize the meat, while sayote provides a crunchier bite.

Superfood Leaves

Malunggay (Moringa) is considered a superfood, packed with vitamins. It is traditionally added at the very end to preserve its nutrients.
