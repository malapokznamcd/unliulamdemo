Bicol Express

A creamy, savory, and spicy Filipino stew made from bite-sized pieces of pork simmeredCooked gently in liquid just below the boiling point to soften and absorb flavors. in rich coconut milk and shrimp paste. Balanced with ginger and a generous amount of chili peppers, this dish is a bold explosion of flavors.

Tender pork belly strips bathed in spicy, creamy coconut sauce.
1 HR Total Time
Easy Difficulty
6 People Serves

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. pork belly, sliced into strips
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 2 cups coconut cream
  • 1/4 cup shrimp paste (bagoong alamang)
  • 5 pieces Thai chili pepperRegular red chili , chopped
  • 2 pieces Serrano pepper, sliced
  • 2 thumbs ginger, minced
  • 1 piece onion, chopped
  • 5 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 8g Maggi Magic Sarap
  • 1 cup water (optional)

Pork

Cook

1
Combine pork, ginger, garlic, onion, Thai chili pepper, Serrano pepper, bagoong, and coconut milk in a pan. Mix well.
2
Cover the pan and turn the heat to medium. Once the mixture boils, adjust heat to low and simmer for 50 minutes until pork is tender. Add water if it dries out too much.
3
Add the coconut cream and more bagoong if needed. Season with Maggi Magic Sarap.
4
Continue cooking on low heat until the sauce reduces and thickens to your desired consistency.
5

Named After a Train

Despite its name, Bicol Express was popularized in Malate, Manila by Cely Kalaw in the 1960s. She drew inspiration from a Bicolano dish called Gulay na Lada but modified the heat levels. The name was inspired by the sound of the daily train headed from Manila to Naga—the Bicol Express.

The Bagoong Backbone

The shrimp paste is the primary source of salt and umami. It provides a savory depth that balances the natural sweetness of the coconut milk.

Heat Management

Thai chili peppers are the source of the heat. If you prefer a milder version, you can remove the seeds or reduce the number of chilies used.

The Cream Finish

Adding coconut cream toward the end of the cooking process ensures a richer, thicker sauce compared to using just coconut milk alone.
