Filipino Chicken Adobo

The unofficial national dish of the Philippines. This iconic recipe features chicken marinated and simmered in a perfectly balanced mix of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and peppercorns for a savory, tangy, and slightly sweet flavor profile.

Tender chicken pieces glazed in a rich, garlicky soy-vinegar sauce.
40 MINS Total Time
Easy Difficulty
4 People Serves

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 3 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 4 tbsp soy sauce
  • 6 tbsp white vinegar
  • 5 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • 3 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp whole peppercorn

The Pride of the Philippines

Adobo is more than just a recipe; it's a cooking method. Derived from the Spanish word adobar (to marinate), Filipinos have been using vinegar and salt to preserve meat long before Spanish colonization. Today, it is the most recognizable Filipino dish worldwide.

The Vinegar Rule

A common tip is to never stir the vinegar immediately after pouring it in. Let it boil for a few minutes to "cook out" the raw harshness of the acid.

Better with Age

Adobo is famous for tasting even better the next day! The vinegar acts as a preservative and the meat continues to absorb the flavors overnight.

Variations

While soy sauce is standard (Adobong Itim), some regions prefer "Adobong Puti" which uses only salt and vinegar for a cleaner, sharper taste.
