Ginisang Munggo

A comforting and nutritious Filipino mung bean stew. This dish features savory sautéed aromatics, tender mung beans, and leafy greens, often topped with crispy chicharon for an added crunch.

Thick and creamy mung bean soup with pork and spinach.
1 HR 20 MINS Total Time
Easy Difficulty
4-6 People Serves

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups Mung beans (Munggo)
  • ½ lb pork, thinly sliced
  • 8 pieces medium shrimp (optional)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped
  • 1 tbsp garlic, minced
  • 2 cups spinach or malunggay leaves
  • ½ cup crushed pork rind (chicharon)
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce (patis)
  • 1 Knorr beef or pork cube
  • 24 oz water (for boiling beans)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Pour the cooked mung beans (including the water) into the pan with the sautéed meat. Simmer for 10 minutes, adding more water if it becomes too thick.

Add the spinach and crushed chicharon. Cook for 2 more minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot with rice!

Friday's Favorite

In the Philippines, Ginisang Munggo is famously known as the "Friday dish." While the origins of this tradition are debated—ranging from Catholic practices of abstaining from meat on Fridays to it being a convenient end-of-the-week budget meal—it remains a staple in almost every Filipino household.

The Chicharon Crunch

Adding crushed pork rinds (chicharon) at the very end provides a savory contrast to the creamy, soft texture of the beans.

Nutritional Powerhouse

Mung beans are packed with protein, fiber, and vitamins. Adding malunggay (moringa) or spinach makes this one of the healthiest Filipino stews.

Tinapa Variation

Many families prefer using flaked tinapa (smoked fish) instead of pork for a deeper, smokier flavor profile that pairs perfectly with the earthy beans.
