Sardines with Misua and Patola

A nostalgic and budget-friendly Filipino soup that brings comfort during rainy days. This dish combines savory canned sardines in tomato sauce with delicate misua noodles and refreshing slices of patola (silk gourd). It is a quick, nutritious meal that proves simple ingredients can create a hearty family favorite.

Warm, savory soup perfect for cozy family dinners.
12 MINS Total Time
Easy Difficulty
4 People Serves

Ingredients

  • 4 oz. misua (thin wheat noodles)
  • 15 oz. canned sardines in tomato sauce
  • 1 medium patola, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 to 1 head garlic, crushed
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1/2 cup green onions, chopped
  • 3 tbsp cooking oil
  • 4 to 5 cups water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

A Rainy Day Classic

Sardines with Misua is a beloved dish often associated with the rainy season in the Philippines. Because it uses pantry staples like canned sardines and dried noodles, it became the go-to meal during typhoons or brownouts when fresh market trips were difficult. Its ability to provide warmth and nutrition with minimal cooking time has made it a symbol of Filipino resilience and home-style comfort.

The Garlic Factor

In Filipino cooking, a generous amount of garlic is essential for this soup. Sautéing the garlic until it is medium brown provides a deep, toasted aroma that cuts through the fishiness of the sardines.

Misua Management

Misua noodles are very delicate and thin. They absorb liquid quickly, so they are added last to prevent the soup from becoming too thick or the noodles from becoming mushy.

Patola Substitutes

If patola is unavailable, many households use zucchini or chayote. These vegetables provide a similar mild sweetness and tender texture to the broth.
