Pancit Bihon Guisado

A Filipino party staple! Pancit Bihon is a delicious stir-fried noodle dish made from thin rice sticks, tender pork, chicken, and a colorful array of crisp vegetables. It’s a savory, "conveniently cooked" meal that symbolizes long life and good health.

Savory stir-fried rice noodles packed with meat and fresh vegetables.
40 MINS Cooking Time
Medium Difficulty
8 People Serves

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pancit bihon (rice noodles)
  • 1/2 lb pork (thinly sliced)
  • 1/2 lb chicken (cooked and shredded)
  • 1/8 lb snow peas
  • 1 cup carrots (julienned)
  • 1/2 small cabbage (chopped)
  • 1 cup celery leaves (chopped)
  • 5 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tsp chicken powder
  • 3-4 cups water
  • 3 tbsp cooking oil

The Long-Life Noodle

Derived from the Hokkien word "pian e sit" (meaning something conveniently cooked), Pancit was introduced by Chinese immigrants. Over centuries, it became localized with Filipino ingredients like calamansi and local vegetables. Bihon specifically refers to the thin rice sticks used in this stir-fry.

Don't Cut the Noodles

Superstition says you should never cut the noodles when cooking or serving, as long noodles represent a long and healthy life.

The Secret Squeeze

Pancit is incomplete without a side of fresh Calamansi. The citrus acidity cuts through the saltiness of the soy sauce perfectly.
