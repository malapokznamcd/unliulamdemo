glossary


Roux
A mixture of fat and flour used as a thickening agent for sauces.

Batter
A liquid or semi-liquid mixture—typically consisting of flour, eggs, and milk—used to coat food before frying or as a base for baked goods.

Whisk
To beat ingredients together with a rapid, circular motion to incorporate air, remove lumps, or blend liquids into a smooth mixture.

Air Fry
To cook food by circulating extremely hot air at high speeds, creating a crispy exterior similar to deep-frying using little to no oil.

Evaporates
The process where liquid turns into gas due to heat, reducing the volume of a dish while intensifying its flavors and thickness.

Sear
To brown the surface of food quickly at a very high temperature to develop a complex, savory crust and deep flavor.

sliced
To cut food into thin, flat pieces of uniform thickness, ensuring even cooking and consistent presentation.

tripe
The edible lining of the stomach of various farm animals, mainly cattle, characterized by its rubbery texture and honeycomb appearance.

Marinade
A seasoned liquid—typically containing an acid, oil, and aromatics—used to soak food to enhance flavor and tenderness.

Marinate
To soak food in a seasoned liquid (acid, oil, and aromatics) to tenderize the texture and infuse deep flavor.


Clumping
The formation of dry, unmixed lumps in a liquid, often caused by adding powders too quickly or into a liquid that is too hot.

Pan-fry
To cook food in a skillet with a moderate amount of fat over medium-high heat, creating a crisp, browned crust.

simmered 
Cooked gently in liquid just below the boiling point to soften and absorb flavors.

Fry 
To cook food in hot fat or oil, typically until it reaches a crisp, golden-brown texture.

Sauté
To cook food quickly in a minimal amount of fat over relatively high heat, frequently tossing or stirring.

Steamed
Cooked using the heat of water vapor, typically in a sealed container or over a boiling pot, to maintain moisture and nutrients.

Marinate
To soak food in a seasoned liquid (acid, oil, and aromatics) to tenderize the texture and infuse deep flavor
